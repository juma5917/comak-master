Training

Training in the safe way for workers to do their jobs well is an investment that will pay back over and over again in fewer injuries and illnesses, better morale, lower insurance premiums and more.

Comak trainings utilize a combination of lecture-style presentations, interactive open discussions, practical demonstrations including role plays, engaging video and audio-visual materials, as well as collaborative group exercises. These diverse approaches collectively enhance the overall learning experience for all participants.

Upon the successful conclusion of the training, all course participants will receive certificates that have been officially recognized and approved by the Directorate of Occupational Health and Safety Services(DOSHS).

Types of training

The Comak training team organizes environmental awareness programs aimed at providing your staff with essential skills and knowledge related to the environment.

Providing training to the staff in environmental awareness is anticipated to significantly enhance their capabilities and foster a stronger sense of organizational awareness regarding the environment and safety. This training will also cultivate a culture where every staff member assumes personal accountability for the environment and safety in all aspects of their work.

Training Objectives
  • Comprehend existing environmental regulations and their relevance to organizations.
  • Grasp the significant aspects and impacts of the environment within the organization.
  • Understand Environmental Management Plan(EMP)
  • Learn an overview of ISO 14001

We provide on-site fire safety training for your firefighting team. Legal regulations mandate that every workplace establish a fire safety team, which must receive fire safety training from trainers approved by DOSHS, in accordance with sections 20 and 21 of the Fire Risk Reduction Rules, 2007. Our skilled and certified fire safety trainers will conduct this training at your location.

The training encompasses a range of topics, which include, but are not limited to:

  • Basics of fire and the fire triangle.
  • Causes of fire.
  • Prevention of fire.
  • Fire prevention policy.
  • Ways of controlling the spread of fire.
  • Classes of fire.
  • Fire fighting equipments.
  • Emergency response preparedness and evacuation procedures/Emergency response plans.
  • Demonstration/ Fire Drill
Fire Safety Training Benefits

Ultimately, the staff will exhibit heightened awareness of fire safety and will actively contribute to the following:

  1. Enhanced fire risk management, leading to a reduction in both current and future fire incidents.
  2. Effective handling and control of fire incidents.
  3. Proficient and capable fire response actions and efficient emergency evacuation procedures.
  4. Staff well-prepared and knowledgeable in the utilization of fire suppression equipment.
  5. Showcases the company's dedication to adhering to legal and regulatory requirements.
Terms of Reference

The Comak fire safety training team will commit to the following actions:

  1. Deliver professional fire safety training to staff based on the approved syllabus by DOSHS and DIT.
  2. Furnish certificates that are officially recognized and endorsed by the Directorate of Occupational Health and Safety Services to all course participants upon the successful completion of their training.

Comak First Aid Training imparts proficient first-aid skills to the workplace's first aid team. This team is fully equipped with the necessary procedures, expertise, and knowledge to effectively handle potential first aid incidents and emergencies that could occur within the workplace.

Comak offers Occupational Health and Safety training specifically designed for your Health and Safety committee. This training can be held at your location or at a mutually agreed venue. It aligns with the stipulations outlined in Section 9 of the Occupational Safety and Health Act, 2007, and complies with the mandates of Legal Notice No. 31 (Health and Safety Committee Rules) of 2004, overseen by the Department of Occupational Safety and Health Services (DOHSS).

Our seasoned and certified health and safety advisors will lead the training, following the curriculum (available for viewing through DOHSS) established by the Directorate of Occupational Health and Safety Services (DOHSS).

Anticipated outcomes include the enhancement of staff skills and expertise in occupational safety and health, leading to the advancement of their technical knowledge and capabilities in organizational safety awareness. Furthermore, the training will foster a culture where all staff, empowered by the trained safety and health committees, assume personal accountability for safety in every aspect of their work.

Training Objectives
  • To provide information and engage in discussions about creating a healthy workplace environment, encompassing aspects like gender mainstreaming in safety policies and practices.
  • Encourage a dynamic approach to employee well-being within the workplace.
  • To empower OSH Committee Members with updated insights into the Occupational Safety and Health Act of 2007.
  • To deliver practical skills for effective Occupational Safety and Health (OSH) planning, organization, implementation, evaluation, hazard prevention, internal audits, and other safe work practices.
Terms of Reference

We commit to the following actions:

  1. Organize safety and health committee training on occupational safety and health, following the syllabus approved by DOSHS.
  2. Guide the committee members in conducting workplace inspections.
  3. Create a comprehensive report outlining the details of the training activity.
  4. Develop training materials, including handouts containing training notes, and provide additional resources as needed.
  5. Furnish certificates that are officially recognized and endorsed by the Directorate of Occupational Health and Safety Services to all course participants upon the successful completion of their training.
